Synopsis

Solace is an intimate exploration of grief, memory, and the quiet struggle to keep living after unthinkable loss. After discovering her boyfriend moments after his suicide, Ashley is left with a haunting emptiness—and the terrifying inability to remember his face. In weekly sessions with her therapist, Dr. Kace, she unravels the weight of addiction, love, and unresolved guilt. As Ashley recounts their story, the film gently weaves between memory and silence, pain and healing. In her search to remember him, she ultimately learns how to remember herself. And in that clarity, his face finally returns—not as a wound, but as a quiet gift.
